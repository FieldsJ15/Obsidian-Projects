# PET — Positron Emission Tomography

## Physics
- Radiotracer emits positron → annihilation with electron → two 511 keV gamma rays (180° apart)
- Coincidence detection → localizes activity
- Common tracer: **FDG** (glucose analogue) — tumors metabolize more glucose

## Clinical Uses
- **Oncology** — tumor detection, staging, treatment response → [[MLC-Sim]]
- **Neurology** — Alzheimer's, epilepsy, dopamine system
- **Cardiology** — myocardial viability

## PET/CT and PET/MRI
- Always fused with anatomical modality
- PET/CT: most common in clinical practice
- PET/MRI: better soft tissue, less radiation
