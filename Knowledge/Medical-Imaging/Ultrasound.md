# Ultrasound

## Physics
- High frequency sound waves (2–18 MHz) reflect off tissue interfaces
- Piezoelectric transducer — sends and receives
- No ionizing radiation

## Modes
- **B-mode** — 2D grayscale anatomy
- **Doppler** — blood flow velocity and direction
- **3D/4D** — volumetric, fetal imaging
- **HIFU** — High Intensity Focused Ultrasound (therapeutic, not imaging)

## Clinical Uses
- Obstetrics (fetal monitoring)
- Cardiac (echocardiography)
- Abdomen: liver, gallbladder, kidneys
- Vascular: carotid, DVT
- Guided procedures: biopsies, nerve blocks
- Point-of-care (POCUS)

## Focused Ultrasound (Therapeutic)
- FUS / HIFU — non-invasive tissue ablation
- Emerging: FUS neuromodulation → [[Neuromodulation]]
