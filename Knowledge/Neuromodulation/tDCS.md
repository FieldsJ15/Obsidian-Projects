# tDCS — Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation

## Mechanism
- Weak DC current (1–2 mA) via scalp electrodes
- **Anodal** → increases cortical excitability
- **Cathodal** → decreases cortical excitability
- Modulates resting membrane potential (not action potentials directly)

## vs TMS
| | tDCS | [[TMS]] |
|---|---|---|
| Mechanism | Subthreshold modulation | Suprathreshold stimulation |
| Cost | Very low (~$30 DIY possible) | High ($50k+ device) |
| Focality | Low | High |
| Online effects | Yes (during task) | Yes |
| Sham blinding | Easy | Harder |

## Protocols
- Session: 20–30 min, 1–2 mA
- Electrode placement: 10-20 EEG system coordinates
- HD-tDCS: smaller ring electrodes, more focal

## Clinical & Research Uses
- Depression, chronic pain (limited evidence)
- Cognitive enhancement research (working memory, attention)
- Stroke rehab
- Learning and neuroplasticity studies
